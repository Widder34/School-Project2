Youth Workshop on Mosquito Safety

A youth workshop was held to teach students and volunteers how to stay safe from mosquito diseases. The event showed simple ways to prevent illness and encouraged young people to help their communities.

Workshop Overview

The workshop took place at the local community center with over 50 students. Experts explained how mosquitoes spread diseases like dengue and malaria. They also showed how to find places where mosquitoes breed.

  • Find standing water where mosquitoes live
  • Use mosquito nets and repellents properly
  • Know symptoms and when to see a doctor

Hands-On Activities

Participants did exercises like checking yards for mosquito water, practicing control methods, and making posters to teach neighbors. This helped them learn better and work together.

Helping the Community

The goal was to help young people share what they learned. Students were taught to organize small talks, give out flyers, and work with neighbors to remove mosquito breeding spots.

Expert Advice

Professor Kabirul Bashar said: "Young people can help a lot in stopping mosquito diseases. Teaching them means the whole community will learn faster."

Future Plans

Organizers want to hold more workshops in other schools and areas. They hope more young people will learn and help their communities stay healthy and safe.

Key Points

  • Youth can help keep communities healthy.
  • Doing activities helps learning stick.
  • Working together and sharing knowledge stops diseases.
